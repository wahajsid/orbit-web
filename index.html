The problem

Nobody started a business to do bookkeeping.

Founders dodge it. Finance teams drown in it. And the tools built for accountants quietly assume you are one.

01

Coding is a slog

Every receipt gets filed to the right account by hand. Get it wrong and the numbers quietly lie to you. Xero and QuickBooks just assume you know your debits from your credits.

02

Tax is a minefield

One missing TRN or a wonky invoice format, and there goes your recoverable VAT. By the time the auditor spots it, the money's long gone.

03

The close eats your month

Accruals, prepayments, reconciliations, chasing people to explain why a number moved. Days of it, every single month, just to find out where you stand.

How it works

Send it. Orbit handles the rest.

The hard, boring parts run themselves. You just talk to it like a person.

STEP 01

Getting started

Live in a day. Not a quarter.

Most accounting tools make you rebuild your whole world before they're useful. Orbit starts from what you already have.

01

Bring your history

Export your existing ledger from Xero, QuickBooks or Zoho — or hand us a spreadsheet. Orbit ingests it, learns your chart of accounts, and studies how every supplier and customer was coded before.

02

It learns your habits

That history becomes memory. From day one, Orbit codes a Careem receipt or a DEWA bill the way you always have — no rules to write, no training to sit through.

03

Connect your channels

Hook up WhatsApp, email and your bank feed in a few taps. Start forwarding documents the same afternoon. Keep your ledger where it is — Orbit syncs to it.

Your historical ledger is the secret. The more you bring, the smarter Orbit is on day one — so onboarding is mostly us doing the heavy lifting, not you.
Why we built this

We watched good accountants lose their evenings.

We didn't come to this from the outside. We've spent years in tax and finance across the Gulf, and we kept seeing the same thing: brilliant people buried under work a machine should have handled hours ago.

Coding receipts at midnight. Chasing a supplier for a TRN that should never have been missing. Rebuilding the same accrual every month. Explaining, again, why a number moved. None of it was the actual job — the judgement, the advice, the strategy. It was the busywork around the job, and it was quietly burning people out.

So we built the thing we always wished we'd had. Orbit takes the headaches off the plate — the coding, the tax checks, the close — and hands the time back to the people who are good at the parts that matter. That's the whole idea.

— The team behind Orbit
